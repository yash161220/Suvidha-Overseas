FAQ's

Got any questions? Learn more about us.

Am I eligible to study abroad?

International students must meet specific entry criteria, including academic qualifications and English proficiency, varying by universities. Your IDP counselor will assess your eligibility and recommend suitable courses and locations.

How much will it cost to study abroad?

The cost of studying abroad varies based on program type, duration, travel distance, and desired lifestyle. Certain visas require specific financial proof, and part-time work options are available with limited hours to balance living expenses.

When should I start planning my study abroad procedure?

We recommend starting your planning process early, at least a year before your intended intake, to consider all aspects such as interests, research, location, costs, and accommodation. Our team is here to assist you throughout the entire journey.

How long does the application process take?

We advise starting your application process a year in advance to allow ample processing time and preparation for your overseas experience, considering varying academic year start dates across countries.

What if my parents don’t want me to go overseas?

While it's normal for parents to feel anxious about their child studying abroad, involving them in discussions with IDP can provide reassurance. Our guide for parents addresses concerns and highlights the support provided by a professional organization like IDP in application processes and preparations.

What if I can’t afford to study overseas?

International students can access various scholarships and bursaries based on academic achievements, with details available through consultations with IDP counselors.

Harvard University

Harvard University courses for international students comprise more than 50 fields of study for undergraduates and more than 100 graduate courses. A vast number of Harvard University programs have been ranked in the top 10 courses worldwide by the prestigious ranking body US News & World Report and most of the courses rank #1 in the entire world. Harvard University median class size is 12, which gives a lot of visibility to students and helps them interact one-on-one with the world-class faculty.

Harvard University courses are offered at UG, graduate and research level. Although Harvard is an R1 university, focused extensively on research, the other popular courses offered here are MBA and LLM.

The University of Manchester

The University of Manchester is a public research-oriented university based in Manchester, England. It was created through the amalgamation of two universities – Victoria University of Manchester and The University of Manchester Institute of Science and Technology (USMIT). The University of Manchester is known for its highest standard of teaching and research.

The University of Manchester Research Institute (UMRI) is central to the university’s goal of building on its path-breaking interdisciplinary research. According to the Qs World Ranking 2024, the University of Manchester ranking is at #32. The university has an acceptance rate of around 56%.

The University of Manchester is part of the Russell Group of Universities, along with many facilities and a range of courses. The university focuses on world-class research and the best experience for international students along with social responsibility.

Technical University of Munich

Established in 1868, Technical University of Munich is one of the top public universities located in Munich, Germany. It is not just one of the top universities in Germany, but also a part of the top 30 universities in the world. When it comes to Technical University of Munich rankings, it ranks #30 in World University Rankings in Times Higher Education (THE) 2024. Prospective international students of Technical University of Munich get to choose among 180 degree programs taught in the university.

For Technical University of Munich admissions, international students must meet the English proficiency requirements as well as the entry requirements. Technical University of Munich acceptance rate stands at merely 8%, which indicates that securing admission in this university is extremely challenging.

University of Otago

The University of Otago was established in 1869 under an act of the Otago Provincial Council. Initially, the university was provided with 100,000 acres of land and the authority to award degrees in medicine, arts, music, and law. The university started operations in July 1871 with just three faculty members and originally used a building on the site of John Wickliffe House located on Princess Street. The University of Otago fees for a UG course is INR 18 L and for a PG course, it is INR 31 L.

The University of Otago has an acceptance rate of around 65%. Currently, the university has been divided into four academic departments; Otago Business School, Division of Health Sciences, Division of Humanities, and Division of Sciences.
